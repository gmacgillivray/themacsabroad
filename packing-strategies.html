Packing Strategies

Our best packing tips and strategies accumulated through years of extensive business and leisure travel.

Luggage

City Travel: Tumi roller bags for urban destinations
Off-Trail: Osprey backpacks for adventure destinations
Always Include: Day packs for daily excursions

GMac's Efficiency Method

Prioritizes versatile, multi-use items including wool shirts and stretchy athletic fabrics. For two-week trips:

  • 4 t-shirts
  • 2 long-sleeve shirts
  • 1 dress shirt
  • 1 sweater
  • 3 shorts
  • Exercise clothes
  • Jeans
  • Travel pants
  • 10-14 underwear
  • 6-8 wool socks
  • Rain shell
  • Hat, sunglasses, gloves
  • Shoes for multiple activities
  • Toiletries

CMac's Flexible Approach

Packs based on suitcase space availability with emphasis on versatility over maximum efficiency. Sometimes it's okay to have options!

Essential Items

  • Electronics and chargers
  • Phone tripod
  • Waterproof phone case
  • Headlamps
  • Hand sanitizers
  • Wet wipes
  • Credit cards from different issuers
  • Rain ponchos

Medical Kit

  • Ibuprofen
  • Imodium
  • Gravol
  • Benadryl
  • Sunscreen
  • Anti-malaria medication (when needed)

Travel Wisdom

Key learnings from our years of travel:

  • Create personalized itineraries rather than fully arranged tours
  • Prioritize local food experiences through organized food tours
  • Pack layers for elevation changes
  • Verify tipping customs beforehand
  • Maintain quality accommodations despite daily activities
  • Carry multiple credit cards from different issuers
  • Limit city stays to three days maximum
  • Book flexibly cancellable lodging when possible
  • Spend minimum two-three days in non-urban locations
  • Choose airports with baggage-check services for international flights
  • Avoid restaurants featuring food photographs
