Travel Tips and Resources

We've accumulated years of travel expertise through extensive business and leisure travel. Here we share our best tips for itinerary planning, packing strategies, and practical lessons learned along the way.

Itinerary Building

We use Google Sheets to draft flexible itineraries containing dates, transportation, accommodations, and activities. We emphasize maintaining structure while preserving scheduling flexibility within destinations.

Research Methods

We rely on Tripadvisor and Google for initial research, preferring "Traveller Ranked" sorting. Here are two key evaluation techniques we use:

The Curve

Recognizing that review counts affect ratings, we identify underrated gems further down lists with fewer but positive reviews. Sometimes the best experiences aren't at the top of the list.

Worst Reviews Analysis

Examining poor ratings reveals consistent problem themes. Quality venues show no patterns in negative feedback - if all the bad reviews mention different things, it's usually a good sign.

Booking Philosophy

We balance advance booking discounts with itinerary flexibility. We leverage hotel rewards programs (specifically Marriott Rewards) and airline frequent flyer programs (Star Alliance with Air Canada) when advantageous, while preferring boutique hotels in smaller destinations.

Packing Strategies

Luggage

City Travel: Tumi roller bags for urban destinations
Off-Trail: Osprey backpacks for adventure destinations
Always Include: Day packs for daily excursions

GMac's Efficiency Method

Prioritizes versatile, multi-use items including wool shirts and stretchy athletic fabrics. For two-week trips:

  • 4 t-shirts
  • 2 long-sleeve shirts
  • 1 dress shirt
  • 1 sweater
  • 3 shorts
  • Exercise clothes
  • Jeans
  • Travel pants
  • 10-14 underwear
  • 6-8 wool socks
  • Rain shell
  • Hat, sunglasses, gloves
  • Shoes for multiple activities
  • Toiletries

CMac's Flexible Approach

Packs based on suitcase space availability with emphasis on versatility over maximum efficiency. Sometimes it's okay to have options!

Essential Items

  • Electronics and chargers
  • Phone tripod
  • Waterproof phone case
  • Headlamps
  • Hand sanitizers
  • Wet wipes
  • Credit cards from different issuers
  • Rain ponchos

Medical Kit

  • Ibuprofen
  • Imodium
  • Gravol
  • Benadryl
  • Sunscreen
  • Anti-malaria medication (when needed)

Travel Wisdom

Key learnings from our years of travel:

  • Create personalized itineraries rather than fully arranged tours
  • Prioritize local food experiences through organized food tours
  • Pack layers for elevation changes
  • Verify tipping customs beforehand
  • Maintain quality accommodations despite daily activities
  • Carry multiple credit cards from different issuers
  • Limit city stays to three days maximum
  • Book flexibly cancellable lodging when possible
  • Spend minimum two-three days in non-urban locations
  • Choose airports with baggage-check services for international flights
  • Avoid restaurants featuring food photographs

Pro Tip

The best travel experiences often come from the unexpected moments and local interactions. While planning is important, leave room for spontaneity and flexibility in your itinerary.
